Truckin' Good

User Research, User Testing, & User Interface

June 2016

Truckin’ Good is a mobile app that helps users find nearby food trucks; they can browse food truck's menus, leave a review, and even order straight from the app.

The popularity of food trucks is on the rise and is trending throughout major cities. People use food apps (i.e. Yelp, Google Search, etc.) to hunt down particular restaurants; however, many of those apps do not specialize in food trucks - their searches are very broad and sometimes lack variety of food options.

The goal of this project is to narrow down and specialize food searching to just food trucks. With this app, you will be able to:

  • Locate nearby food trucks
  • See location for any date
  • Browse menus
  • Order from participating trucks

Research & Discovery

Create User Survey
The first crucial step is to create a survey to get to know and understand the potential users of this app.

Survey Results

  • 76.9% say they use food applications to look up restaurants;
  • 100% say they use their phones to look up places to eat; while 50% say they switch between their phones and laptops;
  • 73.1% say they use Yelp to look up restaurants and they love that it's "easy and convenient" and the "accessabilty to a lot of information quickly";
  • Many users believe that some of the negative sides of these apps is that they have "inaccurate information regarding the restaurants" and that "not all restaurants are listed or shown";
  • Many of the users choose one app over the other due to honest reviews, accurate information, and bonus points if they have free delivery options and/or promo codes;

Survey Conclusions
Taking all of this information in, Truckin’ Good needs to have updated and accurate information where people can leave honest reviews for future customers to view. It needs to be convenient, easy to use, and offer a lot of information quickly.

Competition

The main competitors for Truckin’ Good would include some of the most popular food applications out there today; Yelp, Eat24, and GrubHub. I also wanted to include specialized food truck applications that are on the market today. After looking these up, I realized that none of those apps had a solid number of users and if they did, the users would rate that application pretty low due to complications with the app.

Brand Identity

To get a clever brand name, I began by making a web diagram with words branching off of "food truck".Since the name of the app is playful, I wanted to include two types of fonts. One to feed off on that playfulness, the other to have a more serious tone and be appealing to the eye with any length of text.
The color palette I chose is iniviting and warm on any size screen. I also chose a nice neutral green is the action color to enhance any call to action items.

Low Fidelity Wireframes

After coming up with the brand and completing part of the user research, I was ready to begin creating low fidelity wireframes. With the help of my userflow, I created each screen by walking through each step. This phase helped me to organize information for mobile apps. Low fidelity wireframes were created within Balsamiq.

After concluding an A/B test, I increased the size of the map to take up the whole screen and changed the look of the buttons from the wireframes.

Navigation is a very important feature in this app so I wanted users to have access to it at all times. Putting this at the bottom of the screen makes it consistently easy to use.

Conclusion

Truckin’ Good was a fun, yet challenging undertaking. I'm walking away with great rewards that I can apply to any and all of my future endeavors.
I built this with users in mind from start to finish. The initial user research gave me foundation and a thorough understanding of what they want out of this app. I invested a lot of time and energy conducting user research and user testing. I made sure to be very deliberate with my findings and that gave me a lot of confidence to move forward with a solid prototype.
Even with that being said, I do believe that an area for development and improvement would be in usability testing. I would've loved to engage in more face-to-face testing with potential users to really narrow down on the userflows and the functionality of the app compared to other food apps on the market.
