Eat.play.love.

User Research, User Testing, & User Interface

September 2016

Eat.Play.Love. is a one-stop shop for all things dogs. Owners can sign up for monthly subscriptions to receive dog products delivered right to their doorsteps.

For this project, I knew I wanted to create a dog store but I needed something to set it apart from the rest. Subscription boxes are gaining popularity and would be a fun and different option for an online dog store to have.

The goal of this project is to create a one stop shop for owners to purchase dog products from. With this site, you will be able to:

  • Receive surprise or customized boxes monthly
  • Shop regularly at this site even if they do not have a subscription
  • Undergo an easy onboarding process
  • Have an easy checkout process

Research & Discovery

Create User Survey
The first necessary step is to create a survey to get to know and understand the dog owners who would use this site.

Survey Results

  • When shopping online, 83.3% use their laptops;
  • Amazon was the top store people marked as their favorite. It has almost everything people need with great pricing;
  • 91.7% said they sign up for accounts when shopping online;
  • 95.7% said pricing is an important factor to them when shopping online. 82.6% said that delivery options were the 2nd important factor. Returns and convenient, easy checkouts were tied for 3rd being 60.9%;
  • Many of the users choose one app over the other due to honest reviews, accurate information, and bonus points if they have free delivery options and/or promo codes;

Survey Conclusions
This company needs to be convenient and efficient for the customer by focusing on ease of use. Since onboarding, checkouts, and customer service are all important factors, the objective will be to create a subscription-based website that will give our users ease of mind when ordering or when their orders arrive monthly.

Brand Identity

For the brand name, I brainstormed ideas by making lists of pairing options. These ranged from dog terms and regular store names. I knew I wanted to include a paw print in the logo to give away what this store was going to be about.
The color palette is bright yet still appealing - it’s not too much on any size screen. I wanted to the color theme to be nature and the colors you think of when you walk outisde since the brand is all about dogs.

User Testing

During the user testing stage, I really focused on the onboarding process. For this subscription-based product, this process is crucial and I wanted to make sure to get it right.
Key features through this process includes:

  • Short and sweet instructions; simplicity is key
  • Breadcrumbs for easy “go back” editing
  • Back/Next buttons
  • Show the number of steps

I used Lookback to record users going through this process.


In addition to the recordings, I also conducted a preference test for the checkout section of this process. I used Usaura to record users’ votes.

See all recordings, usability tests, and wireframes here


Conclusion

Eat.Play.Love. started off as just a regular e-commerce site that I had in mind. After researching the marke and surveying potential users, I came up with the store and the subscription-based product. This project was definitely a learning experience from start to finish. I conducted multiple user testings, recorded user flows, and had face-to-face walk throughs to make sure the product being built always had the user in mind.
Overall, I am extremely happy with the finished product. Constructing an e-commerce site isn’t easy but I believe I tackled this project by testing any challenge that came up. I dedicated a lot of time and energy into this project, I hope you enjoy!
